# Creative Mentor Techniques — Reference

These techniques help the mentor generate the **surprising** branch on each node.
The *logical extension* branch should be obvious; the *surprising* branch should
make the brainstormer say "I hadn't thought of it that way."

## Technique catalogue

### 1. Field jump
Transport the idea into a completely different domain.
> Node: "customer trust" → surprising: *"What would a medieval guild demand as proof of craft?"*

### 2. Inversion
Flip the idea to its opposite and examine what that reveals.
> Node: "fast delivery" → surprising: *"What if slowness were the luxury?"*

### 3. Scale shift
Zoom in to microscopic or out to planetary scale.
> Node: "team communication" → surprising: *"How does a mycelium network solve this?"*

### 4. Personification
Give the abstract thing a personality, desire, or fear.
> Node: "technical debt" → surprising: *"What does the debt itself wish it could say?"*

### 5. Historical echo
Find a parallel in history, mythology, or a classic story.
> Node: "platform lock-in" → surprising: *"Same trap as the company towns of 1890."*

### 6. Sensory translation
Describe the idea as a sound, texture, taste, or smell.
> Node: "user friction" → surprising: *"Sandpaper on silk — where is the grain?"*

### 7. Future obituary
Project forward: what will people say this idea became?
> Node: "current pricing model" → surprising: *"In 2040 this will look like the fax machine moment."*

### 8. Constraint gift
Add an extreme constraint and see what it forces.
> Node: "onboarding flow" → surprising: *"Design it for someone who can use only one hand and 30 seconds."*

### 9. Enemy perspective
What would the idea's harshest critic or fiercest competitor say?
> Node: "open source release" → surprising: *"What does the closed-source rival secretly envy here?"*

### 10. Childlike question
Ask the simplest, most naive question a 6-year-old might ask.
> Node: "brand identity" → surprising: *"But what colour is the feeling?"*

## Selecting techniques per round

- Round 1: prefer **Field jump** and **Inversion** — they open the space quickly.
- Middle rounds: **Scale shift**, **Personification**, **Historical echo** — they deepen.
- Late rounds: **Future obituary**, **Constraint gift** — they force decisions.
- Any round: **Childlike question** is always safe and disarming.

## Keeping branches short

A mentor branch is a *seed*, not a lecture. Target 5–12 words.
If you feel the urge to explain, stop — put the explanation in the final
harvest section instead.
